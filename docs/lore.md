## 1. The world in one page

The world had one great city, and the city had one great mine. They dug for ember-iron —
the metal that holds fire the way wood holds a nail — and they dug too deep, into something
that was not a vein but a **wound**. The city fell in. Not destroyed: *swallowed*. Streets,
bell-towers, kings and all, pulled down into a dark that keeps going.

Survivors call what's left **the Hollow**. It is not a cave. It is the city, still down
there, *rearranged* — stages of it surfacing out of the dark like debris from a shipwreck
(this is what the player pushes through: act after act, stage after stage, the Hollow
deals out its streets in the wrong order). The dead of the city still walk their routes:
**Hollow Ghouls** on what used to be market lanes, **Marrow Stalkers** in the old
aqueducts, **Bone Wardens** standing guard at gates that no longer lead anywhere.

On the lip of the wound squats **Hollowreach Camp** — half pilgrim shrine, half scrapyard
boomtown. A Notice Board for bounties and grudges. A forge that burns ember-iron. A kitchen
that feeds anyone with gold. A summoning altar where the desperate pull heroes out of the
dark by lantern-light (the gacha: the Hollow gives people back, for a price, *almost*
unharmed). Treasure surfaces too: **Battle Caches** — sealed strongboxes the city's
quartermasters never got to open — and, once in a long while, a **mythic** relic of the
royal line: the *Crown of the Last Ember*, the *Heart of the Hollow King*, *Worldsplitter*.
When one surfaces, every camp on the rim hears of it by nightfall (the global mythic
ribbon).

Why delve? Gold, glory, grief. Everyone at the fire lost something to the swallowing.
**Seasons** are the camp's calendar: each season the Hollow "exhales" — reshuffles —
the boards reset, and the race to the deep starts again.

The four people at the campfire on the login screen are the four ways of answering the
Hollow: meet it head-on (Warrior), out-burn it (Mage), outlast it (Hunter), or rob it
blind (Rogue).

## 2. Character storylines

Each storyline runs the same five-act spine the sim already walks (50 stages per act),
with a personal thread per class. Beats are written as one-line hooks so they can become
stage-intro cards, Notice Board letters, or codex entries without rework.

### 2.1 The Warrior — *the Unbroken* (STR · VIT)

**Origin.** Oswin-kin: a line of gate-guards sworn to the city's South Gate. The night the
city sank, his company held the gate so the market quarter could run. The gate held. The
ground didn't. He is the only one who climbed back out, and he did it carrying the gate's
key — to a door that no longer exists.

- **Act I — The Long Stairs.** He descends looking for the South Gate. Finds the first
  Bone Warden wearing a familiar crest, and cuts it down without letting himself read it.
- **Act II — The Drowned Barracks.** His old company stands in formation in the dark,
  waiting for an order. He gives it: *march*. They don't. The Hollow holds their contract.
- **Act III — The Counterweight.** Learns the gates of the city were never meant to keep
  enemies out — they were counterweights keeping the wound *shut*. Opening his gate again
  might raise a district… or feed the Hollow a fresh meal.
- **Act IV — The Gatewright.** Meets the thing that issues the Wardens' orders: the
  Gatewright, a mason-saint the city buried alive in the foundations, still doing its job.
  It offers him a post. Eternal. Unbreakable. *Unbroken.*
- **Act V — Key, Turned.** Chooses: hang the key on the Gatewright's hook and walk away a
  free man, or turn it — take command of every Warden in the deep and hold the wound shut
  from the inside, forever.
- **Epilogue hook.** Whichever he chose, South Gate's crest starts appearing on new
  Wardens. Polished.

**Nemesis:** the Gatewright. **Fights like:** an anvil that advances — the cluster's wall.

### 2.2 The Mage — *the Cinderborn* (INT)

**Origin.** She was the Collegium's youngest archivist when the city fell — and the only
person who checked out *the wrong book* that morning. To survive the descent she traded
her name to a dying star (the campfire on the beach of the dark) and came back up speaking
in embers. The fire remembers everything. Including what she paid it.

- **Act I — Ash Index.** Burned pages drift up from the deep. They're from *her* book —
  returned overdue, one leaf at a time, each one singed around a single legible line.
- **Act II — The Lantern Test.** The camp's lanterns gutter when she passes. Emberwatch
  takes notice: a flame that *borrows* is a flame that can be called in.
- **Act III — The Stacks.** Finds the Collegium itself, shelved wrong: a library where the
  books read the visitors. Learns the Hollow isn't eating the city — it's *archiving* it.
- **Act IV — Name Day.** Meets the dying star again, deeper than it has any right to be.
  It is smaller now. It is wearing her name like a shawl against the cold.
- **Act V — Due Date.** Chooses: take her name back and let the little star finally die
  (losing the embers, keeping herself), or feed it the wrong book — the one the Hollow has
  been trying to file for a century — and become the archive's keeper, flame everlasting.
- **Epilogue hook.** Either way, every brazier in camp now leans toward her, very
  slightly, like sunflowers.

**Nemesis:** the Wrong Book. **Fights like:** artillery — keep her fed with mana and stand
well behind her.

### 2.3 The Hunter — *the Far-Eyed* (DEX)

**Origin.** Raised in the tree-line above the city, where the lanterns fail and patience
kills more surely than rage. The city never let her people inside the walls; when it sank,
the forest followed it down — roots first. She descends because her woods are down there
now, growing in the dark, and something is *pruning* them.

- **Act I — Root-Run.** Follows living roots down through dead streets. Trees grow fine in
  the Hollow. Wrong, but fine.
- **Act II — The Pruner's Marks.** Finds clean cuts on the deep trees. Not harvest:
  *topiary*. Something is shaping the forest into an avenue. Avenues lead somewhere.
- **Act III — The Quiet Lodge.** Finds her people's old hunting lodge, sunk whole, table
  still set. Every trophy head on the wall turns to watch her leave.
- **Act IV — The White Stag.** The avenue ends at a clearing where the Pale Riders circle
  a stag of bone and lantern-light. It is being *farmed* — hope, antler by antler.
- **Act V — Loose.** Chooses: kill the stag clean (a hunter's mercy that ends the farm and
  the forest's strange new growth alike), or cut it loose into the deep — wild, dangerous,
  and *hers*, the first thing the Hollow has ever failed to keep.
- **Epilogue hook.** Some nights, the camp's watch swears the dark on the rim has antlers.

**Nemesis:** the Master of the Pale Riders. **Fights like:** the answer arrives before the
question is finished.

### 2.4 The Rogue — *the Twice-Hanged* (LCK · DEX)

**Origin.** They hanged him twice and the rope apologized both times. He was mid-heist in
the palace vaults the night the city sank — which means, by his accounting, the entire
Hollow is *his* score: a vault that swallowed itself before he could finish robbing it.

- **Act I — Casing the Joint.** Treats the first stages like a walkthrough. Leaves chalk
  marks on ruined walls. The marks move when he's not looking — *someone is editing his plan*.
- **Act II — The Other Crew.** Finds chalk that isn't his. The palace job had a second
  crew that night. They never made it out either. They've had a century down here to
  prepare, and they remember him skipping out on the split.
- **Act III — The Vault Door.** Reaches the palace vault. It's open. Empty. A note in his
  own handwriting: *"You already did this. Stop letting them make you do it again."*
- **Act IV — The Split.** Learns the Hollow's trick: it doesn't keep treasure, it keeps
  *the wanting of it*, replaying the heist nightly with fresh ghosts. His old crew aren't
  prisoners. They're *re-enactors*. Fortune favors the felonious — and collects.
- **Act V — One Last Job.** Chooses: steal the wanting itself (walk out poor, free, and
  un-replayable), or take the Hollow King's empty throne for a fence's seat and run the
  greatest stolen-goods operation in history from inside the vault.
- **Epilogue hook.** Either way, gold in camp starts turning up pre-stolen: pockets find
  coins their owners never earned, with chalk marks on them.

**Nemesis:** his own plan, one revision ahead. **Fights like:** crits land oftener, loot
gleams brighter, doors forget to be locked.

## 3. Factions

Where classes are *how you fight*, factions are *why* — the camp politics every delver
eventually picks a side in. Designed as a **mid-game allegiance choice** (proposed: unlocks
at Act 2; sworn at the Notice Board; one re-swear per season). Every faction is a real
trade: each has teeth and each has a bill. Numbers are v1 design values, stored in
`GameContent.FACTIONS` as StatBlock-parsable effect strings so wiring is mechanical.

### 3.1 Emberwatch — "Keep the fire. The fire keeps you." (tag EMB)

The lantern-keepers and wall-builders of Hollowreach: ex-guards, masons, cooks — the
people who decided the camp must outlive every delver in it. They tend the braziers that
keep the dark honest, and they believe the Hollow is a siege, not a mine: you don't beat a
siege, you *outlast* it.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Creed** | Endure. Nobody falls off the rim on our watch. |
| **Pros** | `+15% Maximum Life` · `+12% Armour` · **offline cap +2 h** (the Watch tends your fire while you're gone) |
| **Cons** | `-8% Attack Speed` (you fight in formation, not in fury) · `-10% Gold Find` (spoils go to the wall fund) |
| **Best fit** | Warrior, long-offline players, party anchors |
| **Rivals** | The Hollowed (who keep *feeding* the thing the wall holds back) |
| **Story role** | Acts I–II quest-givers; their lanterns are the checkpoints the camp trusts |

### 3.2 Ashen Covenant — "What burns, purifies." (tag ASH)

Zealots of the cleansing flame, founded by Collegium survivors who watched fire be the
only thing the Hollow respected. They don't delve to recover the city; they delve to
*cremate* it properly. Glorious, generous with power, and very hard on equipment.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Creed** | The city is a corpse. Burn it down to honest ash. |
| **Pros** | `+14% Fire Damage` · `+8% All Damage` · `+6% Crit Chance` |
| **Cons** | `-12% Armour` (mortification of the shell) · **forge costs +25% iron** (their rites burn material — the flame takes its tithe) |
| **Best fit** | Mage, push-deeper speedrunners, gacha whales chasing kill-rate |
| **Rivals** | Emberwatch (a wall is just unburnt fuel with opinions) |
| **Story role** | Acts II–III; their pyres are why the mid-Hollow is lit at all |

### 3.3 The Hollowed — "It only keeps what you still want." (tag HLW)

The ones who went down, lost something, and came back *lighter*. They shave pieces of
themselves off and feed them to the Hollow on purpose — grief, memories, years — and in
exchange the deep treats them like a local. Unsettling, rich, and never, ever lost.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Creed** | Give it a piece before it takes the whole. |
| **Pros** | `+18% Gold Find` · `+12% Item Rarity` · **chest luck: cache item rolls one rarity band kinder** (the quartermasters remember their own) |
| **Cons** | `-10% Maximum Life` (the piece you fed it stays fed) · **enemies +6% HP** (the Hollow holds its locals to a higher standard) |
| **Best fit** | Rogue, loot-driven players, mythic chasers |
| **Rivals** | The Last Lantern (you cannot map what you keep bribing) |
| **Story role** | Acts III–IV; they sell the maps nobody else survives drawing |

### 3.4 The Last Lantern — "Forward is the only door." (tag LNT)

Wayfinders, couriers, and the stubbornly curious — the faction that thinks the Hollow has
a *bottom*, and that somebody ought to go look at it. They run the relay of lights down
the long stairs, they carry the mail between camps, and they hold the route record for
every act. Born of the same banner as the famous delver party of the same name.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Creed** | The dark is just road that hasn't met a lantern yet. |
| **Pros** | `+12% Movement Speed` · `+10% XP Gain` · **party presence bonus: `+2% All Damage` per online party member** (the relay holds the line together) |
| **Cons** | `-8% Maximum Mana` (you travel light) · **daily chest cap −10** (couriers log their finds; the route fund takes the rest) |
| **Best fit** | Hunter, party players, alt-account sherpas |
| **Rivals** | The Hollowed (bribes vs. maps), politely |
| **Story role** | Acts IV–V escorts; the endgame's "one more stage" voice |

### 3.5 Choosing (design intent)

- **Where:** Notice Board → "OATHS" tab (future); requires Act 2.
- **Cost to swear:** free the first time; **re-swear once per season** for 200 soulstones
  (the camp forgives, the ledger doesn't).
- **Server-side:** `faction: "" | "EMB" | "ASH" | "HLW" | "LNT"` on the save blob
  (defaulted `""`), bonuses applied in `PlayerStats` exactly like the class bonus block;
  the two non-stat effects (offline cap, chest luck, chest cap, presence bonus) hook the
  systems they name. All five touchpoints already exist in code.
- **Balance guardrail:** every faction's pros must lose to its cons in at least one core
  metric (Emberwatch pushes slower, Ashen dies faster, Hollowed farms riskier, Lantern
  hits softer) so the choice reads as identity, not a solved checkbox.

## 4. How the threads braid (season arc sketch)

Season N: a mythic surfaces (ribbon) → the Hollowed bid for it, Emberwatch wants it
*entombed*, the Ashen want it burned, the Lantern want to know **what drawer it came
from**. Each class storyline crosses the dispute from its own angle in Acts III–V. Season
end ("the exhale") shuffles the stages — and one faction's agenda visibly moved the camp:
new brazier ring (EMB), scorched district pacified (ASH), a marketplace of un-stolen goods
(HLW), or a road sign pointing *down* (LNT). Next season opens on the consequences.
